Multiplayer whiteboard

Redesigned the collaboration canvas powering 60k weekly sessions.

Northwind Labs needed to replace a brittle whiteboard prototype with a dependable workspace for remote teams. I led the product design, rebuilt the front-end architecture, and shipped a design system that scaled with new feature pods.

Role: Product engineer Timeline: 9 months Team: 5

Highlights

  • Implemented multiplayer presence with CRDT-based state syncing and offline optimism.
  • Authored the color-accessible component library in Storybook; reduced UI defects by 34%.
  • Introduced Playwright smoke suites and Sentry performance instrumentation.
  • Coached product managers on structured experiments to validate collaboration flows.

Problem

The previous canvas was fragile and poorly instrumented. Teams lost work during reconnects, accessibility was lacking, and theming was inconsistent across product surfaces.

Approach

I split the rebuild into thin vertical slices: presence, shapes, templates, and exports. Each slice paired UX prototypes with production-ready components, tested in isolation before landing behind feature flags.

Impact

Session completion rose from 68% to 91% after stabilizing sync and simplifying gestures. Support tickets for the canvas dropped by half, and the design system now powers the marketing site and admin console.
